Rules by Which to Name Phage

These guidelines are for new phage names to should be chosen. Please read these carefully before submitting a new phage or your name may be denied.

Rules

  1. Do not name your phage after Nicolas Cage.
  2. Do not name your phage after Nicolas Cage.
  3. Do not name your phage after Celebrities.
  4. Must be a new name. Check the phage list at {websitename} to see if your name has already been taken. Avoid names that are very similar to—or homophones of—existing names. For example, there is already a phage “Mari,” so do not name your phage “Mary.”
  5. Must be 4 characters or longer.
  6. Must start with a capital letter.
  7. Must not exceed 14 characters in length. (I.E., be short enough to fit handwritten on a 1.5ml microcentrifuge tube.)
  8. Numbers and letters only. a-z,0-9,A-Z only
  9. No spaces in the name.
  10. Avoid words that already have specific meanings in the phage world, such as phage, pham, cluster, virus, capsid, mycobacterium, derivatives or phoenetic spellings such as “fage” or “vyrus”
  11. Phage names are case-sensitive, so be consistent with CAPS/lowercase letters.
  12. Do not use a single person’s first and last names.
  13. Avoid all CAPS.
